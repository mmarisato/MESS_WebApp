A Field Guide for the Future

At MESS, we believe that education is one of the most powerful tools in addressing climate change. By making information about sustainable materials, ethical production, and responsible consumption more accessible, we aim to empower our community to make informed choices about what they wear and why. Through highlighting emerging innovations and uplifting brands committed to better practices, MESS aims to foster a culture of collaboration, because a truly sustainable future can only be achieved together.

Resources

Navigating Better Fashion

A curated group of tools and organizations that make it easier to learn about sustainability, transparency, and more thoughtful fashion systems.

Good on You

Rates fashion brands based on their impact on people, the planet, and animals, making it a quick tool for understanding how your favorite brands actually perform.

Fashion Revolution

A global movement advocating a fashion industry that conserves and restores the environment and values people over growth and profit.

Ecosia

A search engine that uses its ad revenue to plant trees around the world, allowing web users contribute to global reforestation and climate action efforts.

Center for Sustainable Fashion

A research and education hub exploring how fashion can exist within ecological limits through academic work and industry collaboration.

Better fashion is shaped not only by the brands and organizations guiding change, but also by the materials that make new systems possible.

Materials

Emerging Textiles

Textile innovation plays a major role in building a more responsible fashion future, from agricultural waste fibers to bio-based synthetics.

Piñatex®

A plant-based leather alternative by Ananas Anam made from pineapple leaf fibers, a byproduct of agriculture that would otherwise go to waste.

Agraloop™

Circular Systems transforms agricultural waste like rice straw and hemp stalks into high-quality natural fibers by upcycling what is typically discarded into textiles.

Microsilk™

Created by biotechnology company Bolt Threads, the company uses bioengineered silk proteins spun by spiders to create sustainable silk.

Kintra

Made with low cost, abundant raw materials that are 100% bio-based and traceable, Kintra Yarn is a sustainable, biodegradable alternative to polyester.
